Profile

Music rooted in identity, memory, and cinematic emotion.

Richard Córdoba is a Colombian-American film composer, sound designer, and music supervisor trained in New York at some of the world’s most respected music institutions. After moving to Bogotá in 2004, he developed a distinctive voice in Latin American film music, collaborating with acclaimed directors including Ciro Guerra, Annie Canavaggio, and Juan Mejía Botero. His work combines Colombian folk traditions, jazz language, contemporary orchestration, and field recording into scores that serve story, character, and place.
